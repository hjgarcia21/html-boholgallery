KNOW BOHOL!


Bohol is the 10th largest island of the Philippines. It is an oval-shaped island province located in Central Visayas. It is bounded by Ceb in the east; Bohol Strat in the west; Camotes Sea in the north; and Mindanow Sea in the south. Tourism on this attractive, idylic and secnic island has begun to develop





CITY AND TOWNS


Bohol is a haven of tropical natural beauty. The coastline fo the island skimmed by gentle coves and white sand beaches. Bohol is well-known locally as a paradise for divers and snorkelers, though it's not as internationally famous as Boracay. The province of Bohol is a first-class province divided into three congressional district, comprising 1 component city and 47 municapalities. It has 1,109 barangays.



TAGBILARAN CITY

Tagbilaran, officially the City of Tagbilaran is a third class component city and capital of the province of Bohol, Philippines According to the 2020 census, it has a population of 104, 976 people.





FIRST CLASS MUNICIPALITY

First Class: The municipalities that have obtaiend an averge total revenueof three hundred thousand or more per annum during the last four fiscal years, and shall have eight councilors.

The First Class Municipality of Bohol Composed of:
Talibon, Tubigon, Ubay





SECOND CLASS MUNICIPALITY

Second Class: The municipalities that have obtained an average total revenueof two hundred thousand pesos or more per annum, but less than three hundred thousand pesos, during the last four fiscal years, and shall have eight councilors

The Second Class Municipality of Bohol Composed of:
Carmen, Loon





THIRD CLASS MUNICIPALITY

Third Class: The municipalities that have obtained an average total revenue of one hundred thousand pesos or more per annum, but less than two hundred thousand pesos, during the last four fiscal years, and shall have eight councilors

The Third Class Municipality of Bohol Composed of:
Calape, Inabanga, Sierra Bullones, Trinidad




FOURTH CLASS MUNICIPALITY

Fourth Class: The municapalities that have obtained an average total revenue of fifty thousand pesos or more per annum but less than one hundred thousadn pesos, during the last four fiscal years, and shall have eight councilors

The Fourth Class Municipality of Bohol Composed of:
Alicia, Antequera, Baclayon, Balilihan, Bien Unido, Bilar, Candijay, Danao, Garcia Hernanadez, Guindulman, Loboc, Mabini, Maribojoc, Panglao, Pilar, President Carlos, Sagbayan, San Miguel, Valencia







FIFTH CLASS MUNICIPALITY

Fifth Class: The municipality that have obtained an average total revenue of thirty thousand pesos or more per annum, but less that fifty thousand pesos, during the last four fiscal years, and shall have eight councilors.

The Fifth Class Municipality of Bohol Composed of:
Alburquerque Anda, Batuan, Clarin, Cortes, Dagohoy, Lila, Loay, San Isidro, Sevilla and Sikatuna.
